# Visualization Guidelines

## Purpose

Use visuals only when they make evidence easier to compare, not to make the report look busier.

## Recommended Charts

### Milestone timeline

Use when Section 1 or Section 2 covers multiple reveal or test phases.

### Sentiment distribution bars

Use for Section 2 and Section 3 when showing positive, neutral, and negative structure.

### Topic coverage bars

Use when showing which concerns or strengths dominate attention.

### Topic sentiment heatmap

Use when comparing positive and negative structure across multiple milestones or platforms.

### Consensus map

Use in Section 4 to show where creators align and where they disagree.

## Visualization Rules

- prefer simple bar, line, or table-based visuals over decorative charts
- label every chart with sample scope and capture caveat
- use ratio or share when cross-platform counts are not comparable
- keep topic names stable across charts
- never let visuals hide missing data or partial capture

## Avoid

- 3D charts
- decorative pie charts with too many categories
- word clouds as primary evidence
- raw cross-platform like-count comparisons without caveat

## Annotation Requirements

Every chart should note:

- platform or section scope
- sample mode or capture mode
- milestone coverage
- any major limitation that affects interpretation
