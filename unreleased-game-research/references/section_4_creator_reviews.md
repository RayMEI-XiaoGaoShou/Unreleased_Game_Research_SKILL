# Section 4: Creator Review Video Interpretation

## Purpose

Analyze a selected set of creator review videos from the latest playable test phase.
Focus on videos that contain actual interpretation, judgment, and concrete gameplay discussion.

## Why This Section Matters

Creator review videos often sit between raw player feedback and analyst interpretation.

They can offer:

- clearer explanation of gameplay loops
- concrete footage tied to spoken judgment
- structured comparison to genre peers
- repeated pros and cons across multiple reviewers
- early expert-audience framing of what the game really is

This section must separate:

- what the creator says
- what the footage appears to show
- what other sources also support
- what remains unverified

## Inputs

Possible inputs include Bilibili review videos, YouTube hands-on review videos, creator impression videos from the latest beta or test, and long-form analysis videos discussing the latest playable build.

## Core Questions

Answer all of the following when evidence exists:

1. Which 3-5 creator videos are most worth analyzing?
2. Why are these videos more useful than other available videos?
3. What gameplay loop or system structure do creators consistently describe?
4. What strengths are repeatedly praised across creators?
5. What weaknesses or concerns are repeatedly raised?
6. Where do creators disagree?
7. Which opinions look grounded in footage and specific examples?
8. Which opinions may be shaped by incentives, weak rigor, or limited genre understanding?

## Video Selection Rules

Do not analyze videos just because they are popular.

A creator video is valid only if most are true:

- it is based on the latest test phase
- it contains clear creator judgment, not only beginner guide or feature listing
- it includes concrete system-level discussion
- it references actual play experience or visible footage
- it is long enough to contain substantive interpretation
- it is not purely sensational or low-information

### Exclude or deprioritize videos that are mainly

- starter guides
- simple news recaps
- announcement reposts
- lore-only interpretation
- summary videos with little opinion
- low-detail reaction content
- purely comedic or meme edits

## Creator Selection Balance

Aim for 3-5 selected videos with diversity across audience size, stance, platform, genre familiarity, and creator style.

## Creator Credibility Framework

For each creator, assess:

- apparent familiarity with the genre
- apparent familiarity with the IP if relevant
- evidence density in the video
- balance of praise and criticism
- transparency about access or sponsorship
- tendency toward exaggeration
- whether they explain systems or only react emotionally

Use the following qualitative rating:

- high
- medium
- low

## Transcript and Claim Extraction Rules

Transcribe or segment each selected video.

For each useful segment, capture:

- timestamp
- original quote
- normalized summary
- topic label
- claim type
- evidence quality
- whether the claim is supported by visible footage
- whether the claim is supported elsewhere

### Claim types

Use one of:

- factual_observation
- hands_on_impression
- design_judgment
- market_comparison
- prediction
- recommendation

## Consensus vs Disagreement Framework

This is mandatory.

For each major topic, classify the creator landscape as:

- consensus_positive
- consensus_negative
- mixed
- weak_signal

Do not erase disagreement for cleaner storytelling.

## Topic Framework

Use stable topics where possible:

- gameplay loop
- combat depth and feel
- exploration and world density
- progression and retention
- technical quality / optimization
- art and atmosphere
- IP adaptation
- onboarding / UX
- monetization concern
- content ceiling / longevity
- differentiation vs competitors

## Bias Control for Section 4

Be especially careful with large creators with likely commercial incentives, small creators with weak analytical rigor, transcript errors, emotional intensity being mistaken for evidence quality, and popularity being mistaken for credibility.

## Cross-Section Interpretation Rules

Use this section as an interpretation layer, not as the factual backbone.

## Forbidden Moves

- Do not select videos only by view count.
- Do not treat guide videos as review evidence.
- Do not quote creator claims without checking whether footage or other sections support them.
- Do not hide meaningful disagreement just to produce a cleaner narrative.
